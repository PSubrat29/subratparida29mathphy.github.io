I am a mathematical physicist dedicated to decoding the mathematical architecture of the universe. My research explores the fundamental questions at the intersection of geometry, analysis, and theoretical physics.

Research Vision

My work focuses on understanding the geometric validity of gravity, the advancement of the modern number theory in cosmology and black hole physics, and the topological nature of dimensionality. I employ advanced mathematical formalism to explore:

  • The intersection of number theory and cosmology, specifically the discrete structural patterns of the early universe
  • The underlying constraints of reference frames in curved spacetime
  • The fundamental structure of time and its mathematical representation
  • The precise definitions of freedom within physical systems
  • The mathematical foundations of quantum field theory and general relativity

Background

I completed my M.Sc. (Honours) in Mathematics from Pondicherry University (2024) with a thesis on Einstein Field Equations. My research spans multiple areas of mathematical physics, from number theory to cosmology, always seeking the deep mathematical structures that underlie physical phenomena.

∮ Current Research Focuses

Cosmology: My current research is situated at the intersection of non-local gravity, spectral geometry, and modified gravitational dynamics, with a focus on the rigorous construction and systematic analysis of fractional vector-tensor theories of gravity. The central programme involves the covariant replacement of local differential operators in the gravitational action by non-local fractional kinetic operators of order s, probing a continuous functional space that interpolates between fundamentally distinct dynamical regimes. Within this framework, I examine the deep structural consequences of non-locality on both static and slowly-rotating curved spacetime backgrounds — interrogating the stability of field-theoretic identities under fractionalization, the s-dependent deformation of black hole geometries, and the natural emergence of effective cosmological terms as integration constants of the fractional field equations. The overarching physical programme is oriented toward foundational questions in gravitational theory: the origin and smallness of the cosmological constant, the microphysical nature of dark energy, and the ultraviolet completeness of gravity as a quantum field theory.

Number Theory: My current research lies at the intersection of multiplicative number theory, p-adic analysis, and the analytic theory of L-functions, with a particular focus on the divisibility structure of arithmetically defined exponential sums indexed by the prime sequence. The central questions concern how such sums behave modulo primorial moduli, and what the p-adic valuations of these quantities reveal about the deeper multiplicative architecture of the primes. A guiding theme is the tension between probabilistic heuristics for prime distribution and the true arithmetic behavior observed when these sums are examined carefully — a tension that points toward genuine structural phenomena invisible to classical random models. To capture these phenomena systematically, I study Dirichlet series naturally attached to exceptional index sets, and investigate whether their analytic properties — continuation, poles, and special values — fall within the purview of the theory of automorphic L-functions. I further conjecture that the asymptotic density of these exceptional sets admits a multiplicative decomposition over primes, suggesting a deep Euler product structure of the kind familiar from the Langlands program. Broadly, this program seeks to understand whether arithmetic anomalies in prime-indexed sums are isolated curiosities or reflections of a coherent analytic framework, connecting p-adic methods with the spectral and automorphic machinery of modern analytic number theory.

Special Functions and Applications: Situated at the intersection of mathematical physics and nuclear astrophysics, this research addresses the analytical foundations governing thermonuclear reaction rates central to stellar evolution and cosmological nucleosynthesis. The work critically interrogates the limitations of classical equilibrium statistical mechanics in non-standard astrophysical regimes, particularly where deviations from hydrostatic equilibrium necessitate generalized kinetic distribution frameworks. Through the deployment of advanced special functions and extended integral formalisms, the investigation constructs a rigorous and analytically unified treatment of non-resonant nuclear interaction rates across physically distinct scenarios, including Coulomb screening, spectral depletion, and quantum tunneling modifications. The overarching aim is to advance the formal mathematical apparatus underlying thermonuclear theory, with implications spanning both high-energy astrophysical modeling and the broader mathematical physics of generalized integral transforms.

Future Prospects: Situated at the confluence of differential topology, mathematical cosmology, and quantum gravity, this work examines the theoretical foundations of non-orientable manifold structures as geometric frameworks for cyclic and multiverse cosmological models. Central to this investigation is the formal analysis of how globally twisted topological configurations impose non-trivial geometric constraints on spacetime evolution, particularly in regimes where classical singularity theory proves inadequate. The inquiry engages the rigorous architecture of conformal boundary conditions, addressing the analytical continuation of successive cosmological epochs across degenerate metric boundaries via conformally invariant glueing prescriptions. A further dimension concerns the representation theory of spinor fields on non-orientable backgrounds, where standard spin-structure formalisms demand systematic generalization with consequential implications for discrete symmetry structures across cosmological cycles. The broader theoretical objective is the construction of a mathematically complete framework accommodating a continuum of cyclic cosmological trajectories within a unified geometric setting, with implications for conformal orbifold geometry, spacetime topology, and the quantum gravitational resolution of cosmological singularities.
