Subrat Parida

Mathematical Physicist

I am a mathematical physicist dedicated to decoding the mathematical architecture of the universe. My research explores the fundamental questions at the intersection of geometry, topology, and theoretical physics.

Research Vision

My work focuses on understanding the geometric validity of gravity and the topological nature of dimensionality. I employ advanced mathematical formalism to explore:

  • The underlying constraints of reference frames in curved spacetime
  • The fundamental structure of time and its mathematical representation
  • The precise definitions of freedom within physical systems
  • The mathematical foundations of quantum field theory and general relativity

Background

I completed my M.Sc. (Honours) in Mathematics from Pondicherry University (2024) with a thesis on Einstein Field Equations. My research spans multiple areas of mathematical physics, from number theory to cosmology, always seeking the deep mathematical structures that underlie physical phenomena.

Current Focus: Special Functions in Reaction Rate Theory of Nuclear Astrophysics, building on my research internship at Pondicherry University.
